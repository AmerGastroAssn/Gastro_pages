Pathway to Becoming a Clinician-Educator

As someone who came of age when trainees often aspired to be “triple threats,” Lawrence S. Friedman, MD, AGAF, set as his goal to become what later would be called a “clinician-educator.”  This slide set, excerpted from a talk Dr. Friedman has given entitled “How I Became a Clinician-Educator,” describes 10 rules he identified in retrospect that allowed him to achieve his career ambition. We hope all trainees who aim to become clinician-educators will find these rules useful.
