Funded by the Takeda Endowment in support of the James W. Freston Single Topic Conference.

Engage with senior thought leaders, examine diverse intestinal metaplasia topics and delve deeper into the pathogenesis, biology and clinical features of intestinal metaplasia.

Attending the 2016 James W. Freston Conference is an excellent opportunity for you to gain a better understanding of how the intestinal metaplasia research community is advancing therapies and management strategies. Our expert faculty will provide greater insight on: 

  • How stem cells contribute to intestinal metaplasia.
  • Potential cells of origin for intestinal metaplasia.
  • The role of inflammation in the development of intestinal metaplasia.
  • Strengths and weaknesses of experimental models used to study intestinal metaplasia.

The keynote address, led by David Tosh, PhD, will also examine the general principles of metaplasia and how they apply to the esophagus and stomach.

For program and registration details, visit www.gastro.org/freston16.
