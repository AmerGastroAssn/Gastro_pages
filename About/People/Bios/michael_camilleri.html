Michael Camilleri, MD, AGAF

Past President, American Gastroenterological Association (AGA)

Michael Camilleri, MD, AGAF, received his medical degree with distinction from the University of Malta Medical School where he also served his residency. He went on to receive his MRCP from the Royal College of Physicians, United Kingdom, and a master’s degree in medicine from the University of London, England. Dr. Camilleri was a research fellow, then completed a clinical fellowship in internal medicine and gastroenterology at the Hammersmith Hospital, London, before joining the Mayo Clinic, Rochester, MN, where he has been since. Dr. Camilleri serves as professor of medicine at the Mayo Medical School and professor of physiology and pharmacology at Mayo Medical and Graduate School. He is also a consultant in gastroenterology, physiology and biophysics at the institution.

Dr. Camilleri’s research interests focus on GI motility disorders, obesity and pharmacogenetics. He has published more than 500 peer-reviewed studies and editorials, as well as more than 150 book chapters and books. Dr. Camilleri has won numerous awards for his work, including the AGA/Miles and Shirley Fiterman Foundation Joseph B. Kirsner Clinical Research Award in Gastroenterology, the Mayo Clinic Rochester Outstanding Mentor and Distinguished Investigator Awards, and the Mayo Clinic Center for Translational Science Activities Education Resources Director’s Recognition Award.

Dr. Camilleri has been actively involved in the AGA, including serving on the AGA Council, the AGA Leadership Cabinet as the councillor-at-large, and as a member and chair of the AGA Committee on Publications and Informatics, the AGA Nominating Committee, the AGA Audit Committee, and the AGA Future Trends Committee. He was the founding editor of Clinical Gastroenterology and Hepatology, editor of AGA Perspectives, and an associate editor of Gastroenterology. Dr. Camilleri severed as president on the AGA Institute Governing Board from May 2015 through 2016.
