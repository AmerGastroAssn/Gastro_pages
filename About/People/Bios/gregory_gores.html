Gregory Gores, MD, AGAF

Basic Research Councillor, American Gastroenterological Association 

Gregory Gores, MD, AGAF, received his BS and medical degree from the University of North Dakota, Grand Forks. He later received training in internal medicine and gastroenterology at Mayo Clinic in Rochester, MN.

Dr. Gores has made a substantial impact on academic gastroenterology, including clinical, translational and basic research. His focus is on basic research in liver and biliary cancers and the mechanisms of liver cell death, and he has been instrumental in the development of clinical research initiatives that have transformed the hepatobiliary neoplasia practice.

In the past, Dr. Gores served as an associate editor for Hepatology and Gastroenterology. He is among the top in his field with awards that include Best Doctors in America from 2005-2010, Distinguished Investigator, Mayo Clinic, in 2008, and the NIH MERIT Award. With a passion to improve the medical industry, Dr. Gores has been a devoted teacher and mentor to over 70 post-doctoral research and clinical fellows for 30 years.

Dr. Gores has served as a professor of medicine and of physiology at Mayo Medical School. He currently serves as the Reuben R. Eisenberg endowed professor of medicine and physiology and executive dean for research at Mayo Clinic, and as the basic research councillor on the AGA Institute Governing Board.
